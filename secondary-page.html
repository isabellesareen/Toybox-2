Founded 2002 Milford, Pennsylvania

Our History

Founded in 2002 at Grey Towers National Historic Site in Milford, PA, Kindred Spirits was born from a simple yet profound vision: to awaken, through music and art, a greater appreciation and sensitivity to the beauty and value of nature. What began as a small series of concerts has blossomed into a beloved cultural institution that serves our community and honors the natural world.

Our founders recognized that Grey Towers, the historic estate of Gifford Pinchot, America's first Chief of the U.S. Forest Service, provided the perfect setting to explore the profound connection between artistic expression and environmental stewardship. Pinchot's legacy of conservation and his belief in the responsible use of natural resources resonated deeply with the organization's mission.

The Early Years

In our inaugural season, we presented just four concerts to audiences totaling fewer than 200 people. These intimate performances, held on the terrace overlooking the Pocono Mountains, established what would become our signature approach: bringing world-class music to an inspiring natural setting while fostering meaningful connections between performers and audiences.

From the beginning, we emphasized educational outreach. Even in that first year, we hosted workshops for local students, inviting them to meet the musicians and experience live classical music in an accessible, engaging way. This commitment to arts education has remained central to our mission for over two decades.

Growing Impact

By our fifth season, we had expanded to eight annual concerts and begun partnering with regional schools to provide in-depth music education programs. Our audiences grew steadily as word spread about the unique experience of hearing beautiful music in such an extraordinary setting.

In 2008, we launched our Artist-in-Residence program, inviting distinguished musicians to spend extended time at Grey Towers, creating new works inspired by the natural surroundings and engaging deeply with our community. This program has resulted in dozens of world premieres and has attracted internationally renowned composers and performers.

Expanding Our Reach

The 2010s brought significant growth and new initiatives. We established partnerships with environmental organizations, creating programs that combined musical performances with discussions about conservation and sustainability. These collaborations reinforced our founding belief that art and nature are intrinsically connected and that both deserve our care and protection.

We also expanded our venue options, adding an intimate indoor concert hall for year-round programming while maintaining our beloved outdoor summer series. This allowed us to serve our audience throughout all seasons and weather conditions, though our outdoor concerts remain the heart of the Kindred Spirits experience.

Youth and Education

Our commitment to bringing music to young people has been unwavering. Over the years, we've provided free concerts and educational programs to more than 10,000 students from schools throughout the region. We've seen children who attended our workshops as elementary students return years later as high school musicians, and even as professional artists.

In 2015, we established our annual Young Artists Competition, providing aspiring musicians with performance opportunities and mentorship from established professionals. Many competition winners have gone on to distinguished careers in music, carrying forward the values and artistic excellence they experienced at Kindred Spirits.

Community and Legacy

What makes Kindred Spirits special is not just the quality of our performances or the beauty of our setting, but the community we've built. Regular attendees have formed friendships, families have created traditions around our concerts, and our musicians return year after year, drawn by the warmth of our audiences and the magic of the place.

We've been honored to present performances by some of the world's finest musicians while also providing a platform for emerging artists. Our programming has ranged from beloved classical masterworks to cutting-edge contemporary compositions, from intimate solo recitals to full orchestral performances.

Looking Forward

As we continue into our third decade, we remain committed to the vision that inspired our founding: using music and art to deepen our appreciation for the natural world and for each other. We hope to heighten awareness of what is fast disappearing and what we take for granted, celebrating the beauty that surrounds us while working to preserve it for future generations.

Grey Towers remains the perfect home for this mission. The estate's history, beauty, and connection to conservation ideals provide daily inspiration for our work. Every concert, every workshop, every moment shared between artist and audience under the open sky or within our intimate hall reinforces why we do what we do.

We're grateful to everyone who has been part of the Kindred Spirits story: our dedicated board members and volunteers, our generous donors and sponsors, our talented musicians, and most especially, our loyal audiences. Together, we've created something truly special, and together, we'll continue to bring the transformative power of music in nature to our community for many years to come.
